# Deliverable Specification

Use this structure for a full China SaaS launch pack. Shorten it when the user asks for one component.

## 1. Market-fit snapshot

State:

1. Product and category.
2. Primary Chinese buyer.
3. Most credible use case.
4. Desired conversion action.
5. Recommended channels.
6. Current confidence: high, medium, or low, with one sentence of reasoning.

## 2. Evidence ledger

Use a table with:

1. Claim.
2. Status.
3. Evidence and date checked.
4. Safe wording.

Use only these statuses: verified, user provided and pending verification, inferred, or missing.

Then list the three highest-impact missing facts. Do not delay the whole pack when useful copy can be drafted safely.

## 3. Positioning directions

Create three genuinely different directions:

1. Outcome led.
2. Pain led.
3. Mechanism or category led.

For each direction include:

1. Chinese headline.
2. Chinese subheadline.
3. English back translation.
4. Best-fit buyer and reason.

Recommend one direction.

## 4. Landing-page copy

Separate this section into public copy and internal evidence notes.

Deliver implementation-ready Chinese public copy for:

1. Hero headline.
2. Hero subheadline.
3. Primary and secondary CTA.
4. Problem section.
5. Three outcome or capability blocks.
6. How it works in three steps.
7. Proof section using verified evidence only.
8. Objection-handling section.
9. Final CTA.

Add English back translations for headlines, CTA labels, and any sentence whose meaning could be debated by a non-Chinese stakeholder.

Then add internal evidence notes covering:

1. Claims deliberately excluded from the public page.
2. Evidence still needed.
3. Questions sales must answer before publishing or prospecting.
4. Statements that require legal, security, or product-owner review.

## 5. WeChat launch article

Write a complete draft when WeChat fits the audience:

1. Three title options.
2. One-sentence summary.
3. Opening of 80 to 150 Chinese characters.
4. Three to five sections with descriptive subheads.
5. Proof or example section.
6. Closing CTA that the company can actually fulfill.

Target 800 to 1,400 Chinese characters unless the user specifies another length.

## 6. Xiaohongshu concepts

Include this section only when channel fit is positive. Produce three concepts:

1. Search-friendly title.
2. Cover text.
3. Opening hook.
4. Five-point body outline.
5. Honest CTA.

Avoid manufactured personal experience. If no real user story exists, use a product walkthrough, checklist, comparison framework, or founder perspective.

## 7. Bilingual sales emails

Write two versions:

1. A concise first-touch email under 120 English words and a natural Chinese equivalent.
2. A follow-up under 80 English words and a natural Chinese equivalent.

Each email should contain one specific relevance signal, one buyer problem, one credible value statement, and one low-friction request.

If the target company and verified relevance signal were not supplied or researched, use a visible placeholder that requests the official source and date checked. Do not invent a company event, pain point, technology, customer, or role.

## 8. Buyer objections

Cover the objections most likely for the product, including:

1. Why use this instead of an existing local workflow?
2. Can the product support a Chinese team?
3. Where is data processed and stored?
4. What integrations work?
5. How does procurement, payment, or contracting work?
6. What proof exists?

Answer only with verified facts. Turn every unknown into a due-diligence item.

## 9. Fourteen-day implementation checklist

Organize actions by:

1. Days 1 to 2: evidence and buyer decisions.
2. Days 3 to 5: page and sales copy implementation.
3. Days 6 to 8: native-speaker and product review.
4. Days 9 to 11: channel assets and tracking.
5. Days 12 to 14: controlled launch, response capture, and iteration.

Assign an owner and acceptance criterion to every action when team roles are known.

## Final quality summary

End with:

1. Ready to publish.
2. Requires verification.
3. Deliberately excluded.
4. One recommended next action.

Before writing this summary, run the reverse scan from `evidence-and-channel-rules.md` and report whether every flagged statement is evidenced, moved to internal notes, or removed.
