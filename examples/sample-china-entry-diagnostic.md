# Sample Conversion QA Diagnostic: China Entry

This shortened example uses the fictional SlateKite Approval Hub facts in `slatekite-approved-facts.md`. It is an independent demonstration of format. It has no client, endorsement, performance result, or market-access claim.

## Decision

Should SlateKite translate and promote its current English website to Chinese enterprise buyers before resolving its support, access, data, and procurement evidence gaps?

Current answer: prepare a limited Chinese positioning test after the evidence gaps below are assigned to owners. Do not publish mainland access, local support, data-location, integration, contract, invoice, or response-time claims until they are verified.

## Evidence snapshot

Verified for this fictional example:

1. SlateKite provides a cloud approval workspace.
2. Users can create approval requests, invite reviewers, add comments, and export an audit log.
3. The interface and support service are available in English only.
4. Prospects can request a product demonstration by email.

Open questions:

1. Mainland website and application accessibility.
2. Data location and cross-border processing.
3. Chinese-language support and response time.
4. Contracting entity, invoicing currency, tax invoice, and payment method.
5. Supported integrations and identity providers.

## Two sample priority findings

### Priority 1: The procurement path is incomplete

Evidence: The approved facts allow an email demonstration request. They do not establish the contracting entity, invoice route, billing currency, tax treatment, or mainland payment method.

Buyer impact: A Chinese enterprise buyer may understand the product but still be unable to determine whether purchasing can proceed.

Next action: Ask the commercial owner to approve one contracting and invoice FAQ before publishing a China-focused page.

### Priority 2: Access and data claims are gated

Evidence: Mainland accessibility, data location, and cross-border processing remain unverified.

Buyer impact: A broad China-availability promise could create an unsupported expectation during technical or procurement review.

Next action: Run a dated accessibility check and obtain an approved data-flow statement. Keep public copy neutral until both are complete.

## Illustrative evidence-safe wording note

Chinese:

`让每一次审批都有清楚的请求、协作记录和可导出的审计轨迹。`

English back translation:

`Give every approval a clear request, collaboration record, and exportable audit trail.`

Boundary: This note uses only approved product functions. It does not claim Chinese hosting, local support, market availability, compliance, or performance. It illustrates how a finding can be made actionable and is not a complete replacement-copy deliverable.

## First three actions in the seven day list

1. Assign owners and due dates to the five evidence gaps.
2. Confirm the contracting, invoice, payment, and demonstration-request path.
3. Complete dated mainland accessibility and approved data-flow checks before publishing distribution claims.

## Full diagnostic boundary

The paid diagnostic covers up to five public pages, a prioritized findings sheet, an implementation checklist, and one email clarification. It does not include a complete landing-page rewrite, code, legal advice, or an outcome guarantee.
